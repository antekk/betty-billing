 0   1 This document, entitled the Schedule of Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery
 0     Benefits ("Schedule"), is established pursuant to the Oral and Maxillofacial
 0     Surgery Benefits Regulation.  The provisions within this Schedule are
 0     subject to, and should be read together with, the relevant provisions of the
 0     Alberta Health Care Insurance Act and regulations, including the Oral and
 0     Maxillofacial Surgery Benefits Regulation, the Claims for Benefits
 0     Regulation and the Alberta Health Care Insurance Regulation.  If there is an
 0     inconsistency or conflict between the provisions of this Schedule and the
 0     provisions of the Alberta Health Care Insurance Act and regulations, the
 0     latter prevails.  Rates for anesthetic services listed in the Schedule are
 0     claimable by physicians, in accordance with the General Rules set out in the
 0     Schedule of Medical Benefits, and are not claimable by Oral and
 0     Maxillofacial Surgeons or Dentists.
 0
 0      1. DEFINITIONS - IN THIS SCHEDULE:
 0
 0    1.1 "Agent" means any of the following individuals who are acting under the
 0        direction of the referring physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon,
 0        Dentist, or consultant, as appropriate:
 0              - an employee of a physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or
 0                Dentist, or
 0              - a hospital staff member, or
 0              - a supervised physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist in
 0                  training, acting under the direction of a physician, Oral and
 0                  Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist.
 0
 0    1.2 "Benefit Year" means July 1 of one year to June 30 of the following year.
 0
 0    1.3 "Consultation - Comprehensive" means an in-depth evaluation of a patient
 0        with a WRITTEN REPORT to the referring physician, Oral and Maxillofacial
 0        Surgeon or Dentist.  This service includes the recording of a complete
 0        history, performing a complete physical examination appropriate to the Oral
 0        and Maxillofacial Surgeon's or Dentist's expertise, an appropriate patient
 0        record and advice to the patient.  It may include the ordering of
 0        appropriate diagnostic tests and procedures as well as discussion with the
 0        patient and/or the referring physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or
 0        Dentist.
 0
 0    1.4 "Consultation - Limited" means a limited assessment of a patient and a
 0        WRITTEN REPORT to the referring physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or
 0        Dentist.  A limited consultation includes a history and examination limited
 0        to and related to the presenting problem, an appropriate patient record, and
 0        advice to the patient.  It may include the ordering of appropriate
 0        diagnostic tests and procedures as well as discussion with the patient
 0        and/or the referring physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist.
 0
 0    1.5 "Concurrent Care" means a situation where in-patient hospital services of
 0        more than one Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist are required
 0        because of the complexity of the clinical needs of the patient.
 0
 0    1.6 "Cosmetic Purposes" means any surgical procedure for the alteration of
 0        appearance which has not been deemed to be medically required.
 0
 0    1.7 "Dentist" means a person who is:

 0           of the following titles: Dentist, Endodontist, Oral and Maxillofacial

 0    1.8 "Eligible resident" means a person lawfully entitled to be or to remain in

 0        Alberta Health Care Insurance Regulations (Alberta Reg. 216/81) to be a

 0    1.9 "Encounter" means each separate and distinct time an Oral and Maxillofacial
 0        Surgeon or Dentist provides services to a patient in a given day (defined as
 0        0001 to 2400).  To be recorded as separate encounters, multiple services

 0        Surgeon or Dentist or may not be a continuation of a service which began

 0        an examination of the patient earlier in the day.  If the patient initiates
 0        the second and subsequent encounter(s) or the Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon
 0        or Dentist is requested to attend the patient by hospital staff, additional
 0        encounters may be claimed.

 0   1.10 "Holidays" or "Statutory Holidays" means New Year's Day, Family Day, Good
 0        Friday, Victoria Day, Canada Day, Alberta Heritage Day, Labour Day,
 0        Thanksgiving Day, Remembrance Day, Christmas Day, and Boxing Day.  Where a
 0        holiday falls on a Saturday or Sunday, the Minister shall designate another
 0        day as the holiday.

 0   1.11 "Own family" means children, grandchildren, siblings, parents, grandparents,
 0        spouse or adult interdependent partner or any person who is dependent
 0        on the practitioner for support.

 0   1.12 "Transfer of Care" means a situation where a hospital in-patient is
 0        transferred temporarily or permanently to a physician, another Oral and
 0        Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist for care and where the transferring
 0        physician, Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist will not be involved in
 0        the treatment/care provided by the receiving physician, Oral and
 0        Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist.  "Transfer of Care" does not constitute a
 0        "Consultation".

 0        problem, an appropriate patient record, and advice to the patient.  It
 0        includes the ordering of appropriate diagnostic tests and procedures as well
 0        as discussion with the patient.

 0  11 FRACTURES - DISLOCATIONS:
 0
 0   11.1 For multiple fractures, 100% of the listed benefit may be claimed for the
 0        greater value fracture (open or closed), plus 50% of the benefits for the
 0        other fractures treated by closed reduction.
 0
 0   11.2 For multiple fractures, the greater value fracture may be claimed at 100% of
 0        the listed benefit plus 100% of the benefit for other fractures treated by
 0        open reduction, or continuous traction.
 0
 0   11.3 A compound fracture treated by either closed or open reduction may be
 0        claimed at 150% of the listed benefit.
 0
 0   11.4 FRACTURES AND DISLOCATIONS - ATTEMPTED REDUCTION:
 0
 0    11.4.1 If an Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist unsuccessfully attempts a
 0           closed reduction of a fracture and finds it necessary to transfer the care
 0           of the patient to another Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist, the
 0           referring Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist may claim up to 50% of
 0           the listed benefit for that fracture.
 0
 0    11.4.2 The Oral and Maxillofacial Surgeon or Dentist receiving the transferred
 0           patient and providing the final reduction may claim 100% of the listed

 0     2.3.2 Cumulative time is calculated by adding the total time spent delivering
 0           patient care as identified in the description of the HSC, over the course
 0           of the day (GR 1.19) and dividing the total time by the time units specified
 0           in the HSC to determine the appropriate number of calls. When the remainder
 0           of the time calculation equals less than half of one call, an additional
 0           call may not be claimed. Separate encounters may only be claimed when a
 0           special call for attendance has been made on the patient's behalf.

 0           the physician must spend the full amount of time stated in the HSC in order
 0           to submit a claim for the service.

 0           amount of time providing the services described by the HSC in order to
 0           submit a claim for the service.
 0
 0     2.3.5 Where time is described as a major portion thereof, the physician must spend
 0           a minimum of half of the time described in the HSC providing the service in
 0           order to submit a claim for the service.  Additional calls for the same HSC
 0           may not be claimed until the full time period as described in the HSC for
 0           each previous call has elapsed.

 0           document the time spent providing time based services for each day of
 0           service (as defined in GR 1.19).  The record must be available upon request
 0           and should be kept in chronological order, for each day.  The total time
 0           claimed for time based services in a single day cannot exceed the total time
 0           spent delivering patient care activities in relation to an insured service.
 0           Claims for services that are described as cumulative time, major portion
 0           thereof or portion thereof may continue to be submitted in accordance to
 0           GR's 2.3.2, 2.3.4 and 2.3.5.

 0           services may not be claimed.

 0           03.05T and 03.05U as applicable.

 0           coordinated, interdisciplinary program for persons complaining of chronic

 0

 0        may be claimed.  There are two exceptions to this.  Firstly, if a new

 0        peer review.

 0    5.2 VISITS - DEFINITIONS
 0
 0     5.2.1 Brief Visit:  Assessment of a patient's condition when history is minimal
 0           and little or no physical examination is included.
 0
 0     5.2.2 Limited Visit:  A limited assessment, of a patient, which includes a history
 0           limited to and related to the presenting problem, and an examination which
 0           is limited to relevant body systems, an appropriate record, and advice to
 0           the patient.  It includes the ordering of appropriate diagnostic tests and
 0           procedures as well as discussion with the patient.
 0
 0     5.2.3 Comprehensive Visit:  An in-depth evaluation of a patient.  This service
 0           includes the recording of a complete podiatric history and performing a
 0           complete podiatric physical examination, an appropriate record and advice
 0           to the patient.  It may include the ordering of appropriate diagnostic tests
 0           and procedures as well as discussion with the patient.
 0
 0    5.3 CONSULTATIONS - DEFINITIONS
 0
 0     5.3.1 Comprehensive Consultation:  An in-depth evaluation of a patient with a
 0           written report to the referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician.
 0           This service includes the recording of a complete podiatric history,
 0           performing a complete podiatric physical examination, an appropriate record
 0           and advice to the patient. It may include the ordering of appropriate
 0           diagnostic tests and procedures as well as discussion with the patient and
 0           the referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician.
 0
 0     5.3.2 Limited Consultation:  Limited assessment of a patient and a written report
 0           to the referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician. A limited
 0           consultation includes a history limited to and related to the presenting
 0           problem, and an examination which is limited to relevant body systems, an
 0           appropriate record, and advice to the patient. It may include the ordering
 0           of appropriate diagnostic tests and procedures as well as discussion with
 0           the patient and the referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician.
 0
 0    5.4 CONSULTATION - APPLICATION
 0
 0     5.4.1 In this Schedule "consultation" means that situation where a podiatrist, a
 0           nurse practitioner or a physician after an appropriate examination of the
 0           patient, requests the opinion of a podiatrist and the consultant does a
 0           history and examination and review of the diagnostic data and provides a
 0           written opinion with recommendations as to the treatment, to the referring
 0           podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician. Consultations may not be
 0           claimed for transfer of care alone.
 0
 0     5.4.2 The need for a consultation can arise as a result of the following:
 0          a)  some unusual or serious clinical problem,
 0          b)  a podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician requires further advice
 0              regarding diagnosis or management, or
 0              both, or
 0          c)  the patient, parent or guardian requests another opinion.
 0

 0
 0     5.4.3 A referral may be accepted from any person; however, to receive
 0           reimbursement as a consultation, communication must exist between a
 0           referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician and the consultant in
 0           the form of:
 0          a)  written communication (consultation request or letter),
 0          b)  verbal communication, or
 0          c)  communication between podiatrists', nurse practitioners' and/or
 0              physicians' agents at the direction of the podiatrists, nurse practitioners
 0              and/or physicians.
 0
 0     5.4.4 Notwithstanding Rules 6.6.4 and 6.8.3, if a consultation is followed by a
 0           procedure performed by the consultant, a benefit may be claimed for the
 0           consultation as well as a major procedure if the time interval between the
 0           major surgical procedure and the preceding consultation is greater than
 0           14 days.
 0
 0     5.4.5 A benefit for continuing care may be claimed by a consultant following a
 0           consultation where the continuing care is provided at the request of the
 0           referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner or physician.
 0
 0     5.4.6 Repeat consultations may not be claimed unless a further request has been
 0           initiated by and received from the referring podiatrist, nurse practitioner
 0           or physician for another consultation. A repeat consultation is not
 0           appropriate if initiated by the consultant.
 0
 0     5.4.7 When a podiatrist sends a member of his family to another podiatrist, a
 0           consultation benefit may not be claimed.

 0   6 PROCEDURES
 0
 0    6.1 If a podiatrist performs a procedure and provides a service warranting a
 0        claim for an office visit or a home visit on the same day, benefits for both
 0        may be claimed only if the services and diagnoses are unrelated.
 0
 0    6.2 If a service is provided in a hospital emergency department, only the
 0        procedural or the visit benefit, whichever is the greater, may be claimed,
 0        unless the problems are emergencies and the diagnoses are unrelated.
 0
 0    6.3 A procedure benefit includes removal of sutures.  The podiatrist who placed
 0        sutures may not claim for removing them.  A second podiatrist who is in the
 0        same practice group as the surgeon may not claim for removing the sutures
 0        either.  However, a second podiatrist may claim a visit for removal of
 0        sutures if he is not a member of the same practice group as the podiatrist
 0        who put the sutures in.
 0
 0    6.4 Anaesthetic benefits for local infiltration are included in the benefit for
 0        the procedure.
 0
 0    6.5 NON-INVASIVE DIAGNOSTIC PROCEDURES IN HOSPITAL
 0
 0        Benefits for non-invasive diagnostic procedures performed for a hospital
 0        in-patient or registered out-patient are not payable under the Schedule.
 0        Payment for these services are the responsibility of the hospital.  This
 0        applies to both the technical and professional components.
 0
 0    6.6 DIAGNOSTIC SURGICAL PROCEDURES
 0
 0     6.6.1 If a patient is admitted to a hospital for the purpose of undergoing a
 0           procedure designated "+", a benefit is payable for a visit provided the day
 0           before or the day after the procedure is performed, but if the procedure is
 0           performed and a visit occurs on the same day, a benefit is payable for
 0           either the procedure or the visit, but not both.
 0
 0     6.6.2 If a procedure designated "+" is performed in a podiatrist's office, both
 0           the procedural benefit and the appropriate office visit benefit for that day
 0           may be claimed, but if a consultation benefit pursuant to Rule 6.6.4 has
 0           been claimed, a visit benefit will not be payable for the day on which the
 0           procedure is performed.
 0
 0     6.6.3 If a procedure designated "+" is performed in a place other than a
 0           podiatrist's office, either a procedural benefit or a visit benefit, but not
 0           both, may be claimed for that day.  A consultation benefit pursuant to Rule
 0           6.6.4 is also payable for that day.
 0
 0     6.6.4 If a procedure designated "+" and a consultation are provided on the same
 0           day, both the procedural benefit and the appropriate consultation benefit
 0           are payable.
 0

 0    8.3 CONSULTATION - APPLICATION
 0
 0     8.3.1 In this Schedule "consultation" means that situation where a podiatrist, a
 0           podiatric surgeon, a nurse practitioner or a physician after an appropriate
 0           examination of the patient, requests the opinion of a podiatric surgeon and
 0           the consulting podiatric surgeon does a history and examination and review
 0           of the diagnostic data and provides a written opinion with recommendations
 0           as to the treatment, to the referring podiatrist, podiatric surgeon, nurse
 0           practitioner or physician. Consultations may not be claimed for transfer of
 0           care alone.
 0
 0     8.3.2 The need for a consultation can arise as a result of the following:
 0
 0           a) Some unusual or serious clinical problem,
 0           b) A podiatrist, podiatric surgeon, nurse practitioner or physician requires
 0              further advice regarding diagnosis or management, or both, or
 0           c) The patient, parent or guardian requests another opinion.
 0
 0     8.3.3 A referral may be accepted from any person; however, to receive
 0           reimbursement as a consultation, communication must exist between a
 0           referring podiatrist, podiatric surgeon, nurse practitioner or physician
 0           and the consulting podiatric surgeon in the form of:
 0
 0           a)  Written communication (consultation request or letter),
 0           b)  Verbal communication, or
 0           c)  Communication between podiatrists', podiatric surgeons', nurse
 0               practitioners' and/or physicians' agents at the direction of the
 0               podiatrists, podiatric surgeons, nurse practitioners and/or physicians.
 0
 0     8.3.4 If a consultation is followed by a procedure performed by the consulting
 0           podiatric surgeon, a benefit may be claimed for the consultation as well
 0           as a major procedure up to and including the day of surgery.
 0
 0     8.3.5 A benefit for continuing care may be claimed by a consulting podiatric
 0           surgeon following a consultation where the continuing care is provided at
 0           the request of the referring podiatrist, podiatric surgeon, nurse
 0           practitioner or physician.
